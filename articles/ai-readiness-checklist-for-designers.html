Am I Using AI Where It Matters? A Senior Designer's Self-Audit

How to use this: For each item, answer honestly. If you say Not yet, you'll see exactly what to do about it - what to change, which tools to try, and how much time it reclaims.

15 tasks  ·  5 categories  ·  If you're not doing these, you're spending senior-rate time on work AI can handle. This audit shows you where.
Research & Synthesis

I use AI to generate first drafts of discussion guides and interview scripts

Saves ~2 hrs per study

→ What to do

Start with Claude or ChatGPT. Paste your research objective and ask for a discussion guide. Your job becomes editing and adding nuance - not writing from scratch.

Try: Claude ChatGPT
Saves ~2 hrs per study

I use AI to cluster and synthesise interview notes or transcripts into themes

Saves ~4 hrs per research round

→ What to do

Upload your transcript or paste raw notes into AI tool. Ask it to identify patterns, group themes, and surface tensions. You validate and interpret - not manually cluster.

Try: Claude Dovetail AI Notion AI
Saves ~4 hrs per research round

I use AI to draft research reports and synthesis documents

Saves ~3 hrs per report

→ What to do

Feed your raw findings into AI chatbot and ask for a structured report with an executive summary. Edit for accuracy and insight - don't write it sentence by sentence.

Try: Claude ChatGPT
Saves ~3 hrs per report

I use AI to run competitive analysis and benchmark reviews

Saves ~5 hrs per project

→ What to do

Prompt AI chatbot with your product category and ask for a structured competitive landscape. Use Perplexity for live web research. You validate and add strategic interpretation.

Try: Claude Perplexity ChatGPT
Saves ~5 hrs per project

I use AI to write persona documents from research data

Saves ~2 hrs per persona set

→ What to do

Paste your research themes and quotes into AI chatbot. Ask it to synthesise 2–3 personas with goals, frustrations, and behaviours. You add the strategic framing and editorial voice.

Try: Claude ChatGPT
Saves ~2 hrs per persona set
Visual & UX Design

I use AI to generate and iterate on UX copy and microcopy

Saves ~1 hr per screen

→ What to do

Describe the context - button, error state, empty state, onboarding - and ask AI chatbot for 5–10 variants. Pick and refine. Stop writing from scratch; your job is now editorial judgment.

Try: Claude ChatGPT Figma AI
Saves ~1 hr per screen

I use AI to generate icons, illustrations, and visual assets

Saves ~2 hrs per project

→ What to do

Use Midjourney, or Adobe Firefly for illustration and mood assets. Stop spending time sourcing - spend time curating.

Try: Midjourney Adobe Firefly
Saves ~2 hrs per project

I use AI to write alt text and accessibility annotations

Saves ~1 hr per design file

→ What to do

Paste component descriptions or share screenshots with AI tool and ask it to generate WCAG-compliant alt text and accessibility notes. Edit for accuracy.

Try: Claude Figma accessibility plugins
Saves ~1 hr per design file
Documentation & Handoff

I use AI to generate component documentation and design specs

Saves ~3 hrs per component set

→ What to do

Use Figma AI or paste component details into Claude and ask for documentation in your team's format. You review and fill in design decisions - AI handles the scaffolding.

Try: Figma AI Claude
Saves ~3 hrs per component set

I use AI to draft user stories and acceptance criteria from design context

Saves ~2 hrs per feature

→ What to do

Describe the feature or flow to AI chatbot and ask it to generate user stories in "As a… I want… So that…" format with acceptance criteria. Share with your PM to refine.

Try: Claude ChatGPT
Saves ~2 hrs per feature

I use AI to synthesise workshop notes and meeting outputs

Saves ~1.5 hrs per workshop

→ What to do

Record or transcribe your workshops with Otter.ai or Fireflies. Paste the transcript into AI chatbot and ask for key decisions, open questions, and next steps. Stop spending an hour writing up notes manually.

Try: Otter.ai Fireflies Claude
Saves ~1.5 hrs per workshop
Stakeholder & Communication

I use AI to draft design presentations and decks

Saves ~3 hrs per deck

→ What to do

Describe the story you need to tell to AI chatbot - the problem, the process, the decision, the recommendation. Ask for a slide-by-slide narrative outline. Then build. Stop staring at blank slide decks.

Try: Claude ChatGPT Gamma.app
Saves ~3 hrs per deck

I use AI to write design rationale and decision documentation

Saves ~1 hr per decision doc

→ What to do

Describe the design decision and the options you considered to AI. Ask it to draft a rationale document. Your job is to check it reflects what you actually decided and why.

Try: Claude Notion AI
Saves ~1 hr per decision doc

I use AI to draft stakeholder update emails and status communications

Saves ~30 min per update

→ What to do

Give AI bullet points of what happened, what's next, and what you need. Ask for a concise stakeholder update. Stop crafting these from scratch - it's low-leverage writing.

Try: Claude ChatGPT Gmail AI
Saves ~30 min per update
Prototyping & Content

I use AI to generate realistic content and data for prototypes

Saves ~1 hr per prototype

→ What to do

Ask AI to generate contextually relevant names, addresses, product descriptions, transactions - whatever your UI needs. Stop using Lorem ipsum or generic placeholders that weaken your prototype story.

Try: Claude ChatGPT Figma content plugins
Saves ~1 hr per prototype

I use AI to write survey questions for user research

Saves ~1.5 hrs per survey

→ What to do

Give AI chatbot your research objective and ask it to draft a survey with screener questions, scaled ratings, and open-ended questions. You edit for bias and clarity.

Try: Claude ChatGPT
Saves ~1.5 hrs per survey
